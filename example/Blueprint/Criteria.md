# Criteria

## Budget

Self-funded by two founders working part-time. Spending is limited to hosting, the email provider, and store fees, on the order of low hundreds of dollars a month. Runway is indefinite as long as costs stay below early subscription revenue; there is no outside money and no burn target beyond "do not lose money on infrastructure."

## Scope Objectives

The objective for this cycle is a **production MVP**: a small team can sign in, save articles, read them, and annotate together, reliably enough to pay for. That ceiling rules out anything that does not serve capture, recall, or shared annotation. No mobile app, no public sharing, no AI summarization yet — each is a different product surface and would pull scope past what two people can support.

## Timeline

Private beta with five friendly teams by end of Q3. Paid launch gated on retention, not a date: the team will not open billing until beta teams come back unprompted in week four. The browser extension must clear each store's review before launch, so extension work leads the rest by several weeks.

## Parameters & Variables

- **Browser-extension store review** — each store's approval gates launch and can slip the timeline by weeks; it sits on the critical path.
- **A second engineer** — if a part-time hire joins, the mobile app and AI summarization move from "different product" to in-scope candidates.
- **Email-delivery provider** — sign-in leans on a third-party sender; a pricing or deliverability change there reshapes both the budget and the sign-in approach.
- **Beta retention** — sustained week-four return by beta teams is the trigger that opens paid billing, and with it the room to spend.
